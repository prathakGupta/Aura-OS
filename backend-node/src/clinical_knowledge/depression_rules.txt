[Nutritional Psychiatry Context: Depression & Low Confidence]
Depression often correlates with localized neuro-inflammation, gut-microbiome dysbiosis, and depleted Serotonin/BDNF levels.

DIETARY INTERVENTIONS:
1. Tryptophan-Rich Foods: Turkey, eggs, cheese, pineapple. Combine with complex carbohydrates (like sweet potatoes or oats) to help tryptophan cross the blood-brain barrier for Serotonin synthesis.
2. Fermented Foods / Gut Health: Kefir, sauerkraut, kimchi, or a high-quality multi-strain probiotic. 90% of serotonin is produced in the gut endothelium; microbiome health is non-negotiable for mood stability.
3. Zinc and Vitamin D: Supplementation through fortified foods or safe sun exposure.

SOMATIC EXERCISE:
1. Outdoor Sunlight Exposure (Brisk Walking): Minimum 20 minutes in morning sunlight sets the circadian rhythm and optimizes melatonin conversion at night.
2. Resistance Training: Lifting moderate to heavy weights induces systemic adaptations, builds physiological resilience, and offers immediate post-workout endorphin rushes that act as a "confidence anchor".
