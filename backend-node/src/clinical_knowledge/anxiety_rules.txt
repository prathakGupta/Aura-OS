[Nutritional Psychiatry Context: High Arousal & Acute Anxiety]
High arousal states indicate HPA-axis hyperactivity, chronically elevated serum cortisol, and depleted GABA (Gamma-Aminobutyric Acid - the brain's primary inhibitory neurotransmitter).

DIETARY INTERVENTIONS:
1. Magnesium-Rich Foods: Spinach, chia seeds, dark chocolate (above 85%), and avocados. Magnesium acts as a physiological brake on the NMDA receptor, preventing glutamate-induced excitotoxicity and racing thoughts.
2. High-dose Omega-3s (EPA/DHA): Reduces neuro-inflammation associated with severe anxiety.
3. Strict Caffeine Elimination: Zero caffeine or stimulants after 12 PM. Caffeine blocks adenosine and triggers artificial adrenaline spikes, mimicking panic attacks.
4. L-Theanine inclusion: Found in green tea, promotes Alpha brain waves and relaxation without drowsiness.

SOMATIC EXERCISE:
1. Zone 2 Steady-State Cardio: 20-30 minutes of brisk walking or gentle cycling at a heart rate of 110-130 BPM. This metabolizes excess cortisol in the bloodstream without acting as a physical stressor.
2. Somatic Yoga / Vagal Nerve Stimulation: Deep-diaphragmatic breathing paired with stretching directly stimulates the Vagus nerve, shifting the autonomic nervous system from Sympathetic (fight/flight) to Parasympathetic (rest/digest).
