[Nutritional Psychiatry Context: Executive Function & ADHD]
ADHD and Executive Freeze states are characterized by a chronic under-arousal of the prefrontal cortex and dopamine dysregulation. 

DIETARY INTERVENTIONS:
1. High Protein Breakfasts (minimum 30g protein) within 30 minutes of waking. Increases tyrosine availability, a direct precursor to dopamine.
2. Tyrosine-heavy snacks (almonds, pumpkin seeds, lean meats, edamame) every 3-4 hours to prevent dopamine crashing.
3. Avoid simple carbohydrates without a fiber/protein anchor to prevent insulin spikes and focus shattering.

SOMATIC EXERCISE:
1. Complex Motor Exercises: Martial arts, dance, or coordination-heavy exercises are superior because they force neurogenesis and demand executive function engagement.
2. High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT): 15-20 minutes triggers BDNF (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor) and forcefully elevates catecholamines, kickstarting the paralyzed prefrontal cortex.
